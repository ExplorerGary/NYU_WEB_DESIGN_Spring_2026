"In the multidimensional context of complex systems today, we must revisit overlooked structural contradictions. In other words, when local optimization approaches global instability, a seemingly reasonable path dependence can become the root of systematic bias. From a broader perspective, we may find that the problem is not the problem itself, but how we define it. Once the definition shifts, solutions inevitably drift away from the expected track. This does not mean we should abandon existing frameworks. On the contrary, such frameworks provide a reference point that can be repeatedly questioned and refined. Without a reference, there is no deviation; without deviation, there is no optimization. In this sense, so-called nonsense is not meaningless. It is a low-density yet structurally complete form of expression that avoids the responsibility of conclusions while preserving the posture of thinking. Ultimately, what we pursue may not be the answer itself, but the illusion of having understood everything while continuously approaching it....
